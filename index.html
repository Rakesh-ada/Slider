Disruption of Ecosystems
Changing temperatures and weather patterns disrupt ecosystems, altering habitats and threatening species with extinction. Some species may not be able to adapt quickly enough, leading to biodiversity loss.
Global Warming
GHGs trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to an increase in global average temperatures. This causes shifts in weather patterns and contributes to extreme weather events like heatwaves, storms, and droughts.
Sea Level Rise
As global temperatures rise, polar ice melts and causes thermal expansion of seawater. This leads to rising sea levels, which threaten coastal communities with flooding and erosion.

Impact on Agriculture
Changes in rainfall patterns and increased frequency of extreme weather events can reduce crop yields. This affects food security, especially in regions heavily reliant on rain-fed agriculture.
Health Impacts
Increased temperatures can lead to heat-related illnesses and exacerbate respiratory diseases due to worsened air quality. Additionally, changing climate conditions can expand the range of disease vectors, such as mosquitoes carrying malaria or dengue fever.
